Micromobility — Dott

GET /micromobility/dott

Real-time and historical positions and statuses of Dott micromobility vehicles in Brussels, returned as GeoJSON.

Description

This endpoint delivers vehicle locations and operational attributes (availability, range, type) for Dott services. Data originates from Dott’s GBFS feeds and is exposed through MobilityTwin.Brussels as a GeoJSON snapshot for mapping, analytics, and monitoring. If no timestamp is specified elsewhere, the latest snapshot is returned; otherwise the closest available snapshot is used.

Data Source & Transformation Pipeline

Data Source

  • Dott official GBFS (free_bike_status.json) for the Brussels region
  • Snapshots stored in MobilityTwin’s time-indexed geospatial backend

Transformation Pipeline

  1. Ingest: periodically pull GBFS vehicle availability.
  2. Archive: store snapshots by timestamp for historical queries.
  3. Resolve: on request, select the latest or nearest snapshot.
  4. Emit: return vehicles as GeoJSON Feature points with operational properties.

Example Application Scenarios

Operational Context for Brussels Micromobility Ecosystem

The Micromobility Dott endpoint provides aggregated fleet data for Dott's Brussels operations—enabling city regulators to monitor provider compliance with licensing requirements (minimum fleet sizes, geographic distribution mandates, parking zone adherence), competitive analysts to track Dott's market positioning relative to Bolt/Lime/Pony, and urban researchers to study shared micromobility adoption patterns across Brussels neighborhoods. The ~187,950 historical vehicle observations (Aug 2024-Aug 2025) represent over 2× Bolt's data volume, suggesting Dott operates larger Brussels fleet or maintains vehicles longer between maintenance cycles—market intelligence for understanding competitive dynamics. For equity analysis, comparing Dott vehicle distributions across Brussels communes reveals whether services reach lower-income peripheral areas (Anderlecht, Molenbeek, Schaerbeek) or concentrate in wealthy European Quarter and city center—informing Brussels Region policies requiring equitable access to sustainable transportation alternatives. The vehicle availability flags (is_disabled, is_reserved) enable operational efficiency metrics: what percentage of Dott fleet is unavailable at any time (maintenance overhead, reservation friction), do disabled vehicles cluster geographically (vandalism hotspots, maintenance depot proximity), how do reservation patterns vary by time-of-day (morning commute vs. evening leisure)? Sustainability planners use current_range_meters data to evaluate electric vehicle operations: average Dott battery range, charging frequency requirements, whether range anxiety affects user adoption (do users avoid vehicles showing <30% battery?). The GBFS standardization enables multi-provider journey planning: Brussels MaaS apps showing all available e-scooters/bikes within 500m radius regardless of provider, with Dott vehicles displayed alongside competitors enabling price/range/type comparison before rental—critical for competitive market functionality supporting Brussels climate goals through convenient car-alternative availability.
