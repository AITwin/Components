Micromobility — Lime

GET /micromobility/lime

Geospatial positions and statuses of Lime micromobility vehicles (scooters, bikes) operating in Brussels, returned as GeoJSON.

Description

This endpoint returns a GeoJSON snapshot of available Lime vehicles, including location and operational attributes (availability, range, type). Data is collected from Lime and surfaced through the MobilityTwin.Brussels platform for mapping, analytics, and dashboards.

Data Source & Transformation Pipeline

Data Source

  • Lime partner feeds (real-time and historical)
  • Stored in MobilityTwin’s time-stamped geospatial backend

Transformation Pipeline

  1. Ingest: pull Lime partner data on a schedule.
  2. Store: archive snapshots with timestamps for historical queries.
  3. Select: resolve to latest or nearest snapshot for the request time.
  4. Format: emit vehicles as GeoJSON Feature points with operational properties.

Example Application Scenarios

  • Fleet visualization on a live map
  • Availability and coverage analytics
  • Urban planning with historical snapshots
  • Sustainability and usage metrics

Operational Context for Brussels Micromobility Ecosystem

The Micromobility Lime endpoint aggregates Lime's Brussels fleet data—one of the largest shared micromobility operators globally, providing Brussels regulators with visibility into major provider operations and enabling comparative market analysis against smaller competitors like Pony or regional players like Dott/Bolt. The ~154,419 historical vehicle observations (Aug 2024-Aug 2025) reflect Lime's substantial Brussels presence, though lower than Dott's ~188k suggesting different operational strategies (Dott higher vehicle turnover vs. Lime longer deployment cycles). For network planning, mapping Lime vehicle density across Brussels reveals coverage patterns: does Lime leverage global operational expertise to optimize geographic distribution better than local competitors, or do all providers show similar concentration in high-demand European Quarter/Grand Place tourist/business zones? The vehicle_type_id field distinguishes Lime's product mix: e-scooters vs. e-bikes vs. e-mopeds (where permitted), enabling mode-shift analysis—are Brussels users adopting bikes for longer trips, scooters for last-mile connections, or mixing modes based on trip characteristics? Safety researchers correlate Lime vehicle availability with accident data: do high-density deployment areas show increased incident rates (suggesting infrastructure deficiencies requiring protected bike lanes), or does availability improve safety through reduced car dependency? The rental deep links (iOS/Android URIs) enable integrated mobility apps: Brussels residents planning journeys see Lime as one option among STIB metro/tram, SNCB trains, and competing micromobility providers—multi-modal integration critical for Brussels sustainable urban mobility plan achieving 2030 climate targets. Economic development analysts evaluate micromobility impact: does Lime vehicle presence correlate with commercial activity in Brussels neighborhoods, do tourists use services enabling car-free Brussels visits supporting hospitality/retail, has shared micromobility reduced private vehicle parking demand freeing public space for alternative uses?
